Personal Information
Born 15 October 1931, Rameswaram
Full Name Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam
Profession Aerospace Scientist
Awards Bharat Ratna, Padma Vibhushan, Hoover Medal, More
Notable work(s) Wings of Fire, India 2020, Ignited Minds, Indomitable Spirit, Transcendence: My Spiritual Experiences with Pramukh Swamiji
Died 27 July 2015 (aged 83) Shillong, Meghalaya, India
Resting place Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam Desiya Ninaivagam, Pei Karambu

ACHIEVEMENTS
  • Aerospace Engineer : From selling newspaper at railway stations, he went on to study Aerospace Engineering from Madras Institute of Technology.
  • Joining DRDO : After successfli completion of engineering he joined DRDO ( Defence Research and Development Organisation ) , a mammoth task in itself.
  • SLV-III : ISRO appointed him project director of SLV-III mission ( Indias first satellite launch programme ).
  • ROHINI : India successflily launched satellite ROHINI in 1980 under his guidance.
  • Chief Scientific Adviser : In 1992, Dr. Kalam was appointed Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister of India.
  • Pokharan-II : India sent out a loud and clear signal of its military strength by conducting Pokharan-II nuclear test under Dr. Kalams directorship.
  • Kalam-Raju Stent : Alongwith cardiologist Soma Raju, Dr. Kalam invented a low cost coronary stent named 'Kalam – Raju stent '.
  • Missile Man of India : Dr. Kalam played a major role in developing missiles AGNI ( an intermediate range ballistic missile ) and PRITHVI ( surface-to-surface missile ) for India. Since then, he became the missile man of India.
  • Bharat Ratna : Dr. Kalam received Padma Bhusan in 1981, Padma Vibhushan in 1990 and Indias highest civilian honour- The Bharat Ratna in 1997 for his excellent service in field of science & technology.
  • People's President : From being born to a poor fisherman family, he went on to serve his nation ,INDIA, as its 11th President ( 25 July 2002 – 25 July 2007 ). He is popularly known as peoples president.
